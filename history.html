Adobe Flash Preservation

History of Adobe Flash Player

Adobe Flash Player was first released in 1996 by Macromedia as FutureSplash Animator, but it was later renamed to Flash when Adobe acquired Macromedia in 2005. For years, Flash was the go-to software for interactive multimedia content on the web.

Throughout its life, Flash played a critical role in delivering rich animations, games, and video content. However, it became increasingly obsolete with the rise of open standards like HTML5 and the evolution of web technologies.

After many years of being a central part of web development, Adobe officially announced Flash's end of life in 2017, with the final end of support occurring on December 31, 2020.

Even though Flash has been discontinued, its legacy remains in many classic web games, animations, and interactive experiences. This site is dedicated to preserving that history and providing access to Adobe Flash Player for those who still wish to experience it.
